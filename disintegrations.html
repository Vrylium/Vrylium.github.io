DISINTEGRATIONS

DISINTEGRATIONS is a collection of eight short, raw, and despairing poems about interpersonal ruin. The collection draws from several genuine experiences, but intentionally does not reveal the identity of the subjects. Exceedingly sad.

1.

How can I arrive
while staring into your boarded-up windows,
weathering your uninsulated touch,
that exposes me to winters
of draft and darkness and dereliction?

No, I cannot arrive there.
I refuse the threshold.

2.

I will either die in her hands
or from need of them.

3.

I,
resident of the dreamworld,
care and careen
suddenly into waking.
What will become of my body,
in the strange bed I arise from,
in a such a different space than where
I last left it?

4.

I curl like a question —
when? —
on the floor of the house I built across
Earth’s long revolution,
deserted at escrow.
They warned me when I first broke droughty ground:
Don’t build here
Don’t build at the well
with no water in it.

5.

This corpse of a year
shall anatomize in reference to the last.
Shadows move through rosy-veined calendar days,
proclaiming anniversaries to a future
with no one to observe them.

6.

Famished, I ate
every morsel in your outstretched palm.
Just enough to sustain,
rarely enough to sate.

7.

My room,
museum, moratorium, mausoleum,
of depreciated relics aching for their pasts;
for the day they were first issued,
when light was kinder,
and the air was newer,
and their worths equalled the world’s.

8.

Through the foggy pane of another now,
another here,
I can see us, faintly.
Through the gossamer parallel,
we are still together.
