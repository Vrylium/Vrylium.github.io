✦ Writing

My primary hobby revolves around writing fiction and poetry. Everything I write is incredibly personal and contains my entire heart, without exception. I've met some truly amazing people through writing, which has been its greatest reward. The soul of writing is connecting people on an intimate and personal level, therefore I believe it to be the most significant and worthwhile craft I engage in.


✦ Video Games

I've been escaping the world through video games since I was old enough hold a controller. The element of participation in a narrative has captured my imagination, challenged my ability, and developed my problem-solving skills in ways other entertainment could never imitate. I enjoy difficult games with strong stories and detailed worlds. My favorite games and IPs are: Mass Effect, Fallout, "Soulsbornes", Metroid, Animal Crossing, Pokemon, and No Man's Sky.


✦ Coding

Ever since I was a kid I've enjoyed modifying the simple HTML of my profile pages, but it wasn't until very recently that I got serious about learning more front end web design. Lately, I've been obsessed with refining my skills with HTML and CSS on the way to taking JavaScript lessons. I feel at home in front of technology and I'm eager to learn more.


✦ Horror

I believe in confronting one's greatest fears and gaining a deep understanding of them. I explore my terror of alienation and powerlessness through sidestream horror genres known as "analog", "gothic", and "cosmic" horror. Each depict nightmares of irreality. I consider myself a horror writer first and foremost by creating works that scare me to death, but also introduce a sense of hope that rewards journeys through horrific circumstances. Overcoming fear, doubt, and pain is how we grow as people.


✦ World History

My fascination with world history began at a very early age when I'd watch historical documentaries with my father. I think it's so important to understand where we came from as a species; to understand our nature, our triumphs, and our atrocities, so that we can be more informed members of society. Never look away and never forget. We need to know these things to see where we are going, because people are still the same animals we always were.


✦ Mechanical Keyboards

As a writer, I spend a lot of time around keyboards. It makes a huge difference when the input mechanism is pleasing to use, so I've invested in three mechanical keyboards (RK61, RK71, and Keychron K2), along with several keycap sets. I've lubed the switches of my hot-swappable RK71 and have plans to buy new switches in the future to experiment with.


✦ Journaling

I started journaling again in January 2022. All my life I've journaled in temporary spells that usually end in the destruction of the journal. In December 2021 I had an epiphany about the fragility of memory and experience, especially pronounced in myself (I deal with derealization). I'm currently using Log+Jotter 3.5" x 5.5" journals; the size makes writing consistently less daunting when you only have to fill one small page per entry. I like to accompany my entries with stickers, either obtained that day or depicting subject matter related to that day. I also bought a date stamp, a Bellroy leather journal cover, and a rOtring 600 refillable pen. Good accessories make journaling more exciting. I only wish I had started sooner.


✦ Solarpunk

This optimistic, sustainable ideal for the future of civilization is extremely relevant in the face of impending climate disaster. Solarpunk champions renewable energy, socialism, and symbiosis with nature. The soul of Solarpunk is rebellious despite its peaceful packaging. It will require a massive overhaul of society as we know it to achieve Solarpunk ideals, and change never comes easily.
